We need your consent to proceed

You have been invited to take part in a research study named "Evaluating Still and Moving Aesthetic Visuals for Cognitive Reception "

Who is conducting the study?

Principal Investigator: Nilay Yalcin (Doctoral student, School of Interactive Arts and Technology, Simon Fraser University)

Who is funding this study?

The study is being conducted by a graduate student at Simon Fraser University in Vancouver, Canada and is not in conjunction with a specific grant or funding source.

Why should you take part in this study? Why are we doing this study?

This research program will provide us with insight into the stylistic animation of virtual characters by determining how humans expect characters to react to them.

Your participation is voluntary

Your participation is voluntary. You have the right to refuse to participate in this study. If you decide to participate, you may still choose to withdraw from the study at any time without any negative consequences to the education, employment or other services to which you are entitled or are presently receiving.

What happens if you say "Yes, I want to be in the study"? What happens to you in the study? How is the study done?

In this study you will watch two short video clips. After each clip, you will be asked to rate an animated character's personality.

Is there any way being in this study could be bad for you?

We do not think there is anything in this study that could harm you or be bad for you. There are no foreseeable risks to you in participating in this study.

Will being in this study help you in any way? What are the benefits of participating?

We do not think taking part in this study will help you. However, in the future, others may benefit from what we learn in this study.

Will you be paid for your time/taking part in this research study?

If you participate in the study, the researcher will give you $1.00 through your Amazon Mechanical Turk account for completing the experiment.

How will your identity be protected? How will your privacy be maintained? Measures to maintain confidentiality

Your confidentiality will be respected. Information that discloses your identity will not be released without your consent unless required by law. All documents will be identified only by code number, and participants will not be identified by name in any reports of the completed study. All data records are kept in a locked filing cabinet and/or password protected computer file.

The data collection will run on a password protected computer located in Vancouver, Canada.

Please be aware that any work performed on Amazon Mechanical Turk can potentially be linked to information about you on your Amazon public profile page, depending on the settings you have for your Amazon profile. We will not be accessing any personally identifying information about you that you may have put on your Amazon public profile page. We will store your Mechanical Turk worker ID separately from the other information you provide to us.

What If I decide to withdraw my consent to participate?

You may withdraw from this study at any time without giving reasons and with no effects on grades, employment etc.

What will be done with the results of the study?

The main study findings will be published in an doctoral dissertation, as well as academic journal articles and/or will be presented at academic conferences.

Who can you contact if you have questions about the study?

If you have any questions about the study please feel free to contact me, Nilay Yalcin, via email at oyalcin@sfu.ca

Who can you contact if you have complaints or concerns about the study?

If you have any concerns about your rights as a research subject and/or your experiences while participating in this study, you may contact Dr. Jeff Toward, Director, Office of Research Ethics, jtoward@sfu.ca, 778-782-6593.

Taking part in this study is entirely up to you. You have the right to refuse to participate in this study. If you decide to take part, you may choose to pull out of the study at any time without giving a reason and without any negative impact.

Do you understand and consent to these terms?
